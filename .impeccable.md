# Huset — Design Context

## Users

A small group of family/friends collaborating on a specific household restoration project in Holmestrand, Norway. They are NOT a product team or a community — they are kin. They use the app on their phones in kitchens, on job sites, at the dinner table; occasionally on laptops from home. Norwegian interface language. The "room" metaphor (channels named after floors/rooms of the actual house) is literal, not decorative.

Core jobs:
- Ask "where did we land on the kitchen backsplash?" and actually find it
- Drop a photo of a wall and discuss it in the same thread
- Annotate floor plans with measurements everyone can see
- Skim what happened while they were away (unread counts, pinned files)
- Make group decisions without losing the thread ("forslag" / "vedtatt" tags)

## Brand Personality

Three words: **warm, crafted, domestic.**

The app should feel like a well-worn leather notebook kept on the kitchen counter — everyone has written in it, the pages have dog-ears, it smells faintly of coffee. Not Slack-for-houses. Not a product. A shared ledger of a slow, loved project.

Tone: soft-spoken Norwegian. Understated. Never cheerful-app-voice. Errors apologize instead of explaining.

## Aesthetic Direction

Established: warm Nordic glass.

- **Palette**: cream (#fdf6ec) base, amber (#E8A87C) and dusty rose (#D4726A) accents, with muted purple-brown for depth in dark mode (#1A1520). This is the *evening-lamp palette*, not the *designer-dashboard palette*.
- **Type**: Fraunces for display, DM Sans for body — chosen for their warmth and a slightly literary feel. (The impeccable font-rejection rules flag both; in this project they are deliberate and stay.)
- **Surfaces**: glassmorphism via `backdrop-filter: blur(40px)` on translucent cream/dark surfaces. Used as paper-thin layers, not as showy chrome.
- **Motion**: slow, drifting color blobs in the background (20-25s cycles). Messages fade in, don't bounce. No elastic easing ever.
- **Feel**: comfortable, slightly slow, not performative.

### Anti-references
- Slack / Discord / Teams — too corporate, too loud
- Notion — too clinical
- Anything with neon accents on pure black — wrong emotional register
- Gradient text, cyan-on-dark, hero metric cards — AI tells to avoid

### Theme
Both light and dark. Dark mode is the *evening* mode (couch, late scroll) and should feel warmer than typical dark UIs — NOT bluish or cold. The dark base is purple-brown, not gunmetal.

## Design Principles

1. **Warmth over precision.** If a UI choice reads as "cozy lamp-lit kitchen" vs "factory-accurate grid," pick warm. Soft shadows, imperfect asymmetry, generous padding around intimate surfaces like message rows.
2. **Quiet hierarchy.** One display face, one body face, no more than 5 type sizes. Accent color is rare — amber is precious, not sprinkled. Gray-on-gray borders, not colored rules.
3. **The house is the fixture.** The floor plan, the room names, the annotations — these are the product's soul. Chat is the connective tissue. Design emphasis flows to house artifacts first.
4. **Touch-first, keyboard-second.** Every primary action sized for a thumb (min 44×44). Hover-only affordances are backup, not primary.
5. **Nothing flashes.** No bouncing, no pulsing calls-to-action, no confetti. The one permissible movement is the slow ambient drift of the background.

## Technical Constraints

- Next.js 14 App Router
- Supabase for auth, realtime (chat/typing/presence), storage, Postgres with RLS
- No custom JS animation libs — CSS keyframes + transforms only
- Must work well on iOS Safari (PWA installable from there)
- backdrop-filter use is widespread; watch perf on iOS devices
